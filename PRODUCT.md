# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, SOC team leads, and hiring managers screening candidates for Tier 1
SOC Analyst roles in the UK. They arrive from a CV link or GitHub profile, skim
for 30–90 seconds on desktop or mobile, and decide whether Zaka gets a call.
Secondary: fellow security practitioners checking the projects.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio for Zakaurrehman Shaikh — MSc Cyber Security (University of
Surrey, 2026), aspiring Tier 1 SOC Analyst. The site itself is the work sample:
it demonstrates triage thinking by presenting projects as incident tickets with
timelines and verdicts. Success = a recruiter reaches the contact section
convinced this person thinks like an analyst, and gets in touch.

## Brand Personality

Calm operator. Precise, composed, evidence-driven — the tone of a good incident
report. Confidence through restraint: the SOC aesthetic (terminal, tickets,
telemetry) carries the identity; nothing shouts. Three words: precise, calm,
credible.

## Anti-references

- Hacker clichés: Matrix rain, green-on-black, skulls, glitch-for-glitch's-sake,
  anything that reads edgy rather than professional.
- Generic template portfolio: Bootstrap/Notion-style dev portfolio, identical
  card grids, stock "About / Skills / Projects" scaffolding with no voice.
- Over-animated demo: motion everywhere, content flying in, anything that makes
  a recruiter wait or feel gimmicked.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the work sample** — every detail (copy, labels, states) should
   read like the output of a careful analyst.
2. **Evidence over adjectives** — show projects as tickets with verdicts and
   timelines instead of claiming skills in prose.
3. **Restraint is the flex** — one accent, quiet motion, generous space; the
   craft shows in precision, not decoration.
4. **Respect the 60-second skim** — hierarchy and copy must let a recruiter get
   the story without scrolling back.
5. **Never break character, never sacrifice clarity** — the SOC framing stays,
   but plain-English meaning always wins over jargon.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` support (already a project
convention — keep it total, not partial). Keyboard navigable throughout,
visible focus states, semantic landmarks, ≥4.5:1 body-text contrast on the
dark theme. Interactive terminal must be skippable and never gate content.
